How we’ll work together

Senior support without the overhead of another full‑time hire.

The aim is to give you clarity quickly, help you deliver something concrete, and leave your team in a better place.

  1. 01

    Fit check & discovery

    We talk about your team, the business process behind the work, constraints, and what “success” looks like. If I’m not the right fit, I’ll say so and, where possible, point you towards someone more suitable.

  2. 02

    Understand the process & define the work

    We map the key steps in the current process, agree where software should help, and pick the most appropriate way of working. From that we define the scope, success measures, and how we’ll collaborate.

  3. 03

    Execute & adapt

    We get on with the work. I’m hands‑on where it helps most: pairing, reviewing, designing, and unblocking. We adjust as we learn.

  4. 04

    Close‑out & next steps

    You get distilled, executive‑friendly outcomes, clear next moves, and optional ongoing partnership if it makes sense.

About

FAQ

Do you still take straightforward day‑rate contracts? + −

Occasionally, for the right situation. In practice I’ve found that productised engagements work better for both sides — clearer expectations, more focus, and less administration. If you can only work on a day‑rate basis, we can still talk about what is possible.

What are your typical fees? + −

Pricing depends on scope and urgency, but most ongoing work sits at around £3,000 per month for something close to a full‑time engineer’s attention, without the overhead of hiring. I’ll always give you a clear, fixed‑price proposal before we begin.

How soon can you start? + −

I usually book engagements a few weeks in advance. If your need is urgent, it’s still worth getting in touch — if I can’t help directly, I may be able to recommend someone who can.

What if we just want advice, not hands‑on work? + −

That’s exactly what Outcome projects and longer‑running multi‑client delivery are for. We can bias towards advisory and reviews, but there will still be a strong focus on real changes in the code and in the way the team works.
